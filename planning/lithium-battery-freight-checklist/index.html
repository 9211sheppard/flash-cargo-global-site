Lithium battery freight checklist

A practical freight checklist for lithium batteries, battery-powered devices, samples, returns, repairs, and electronics shipments.

What to confirm

  • Identify whether batteries are alone, packed with equipment, installed in equipment, damaged, defective, or returns.
  • Confirm battery chemistry, watt-hour or lithium content context, quantity, packaging, labels, and transport mode.
  • Separate new sale goods from repair, warranty, sample, and damaged battery shipments.

Freight impact

  • Battery status can affect carrier acceptance, air eligibility, packaging, labeling, and documentation.
  • A shipment can be delayed if the battery configuration is discovered after pickup.
  • Electronics freight plans should include battery questions before routing is quoted.

Useful inquiry notes

  • State battery configuration in plain language.
  • Flag damaged, defective, recalled, or return batteries immediately.
  • Share product photos, labels, safety documents, and receiver requirements if available.

Planning questions

Who should use this product guide?

Importers, exporters, manufacturers, buyers, and operations teams can use it before quoting, booking, routing, or handing cargo to a carrier.

Does this replace a broker, agency, or compliance professional?

No. It is a freight planning aid that helps teams organize the facts and questions they should confirm with the right responsible party.
